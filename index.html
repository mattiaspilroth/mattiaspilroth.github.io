Designing resilient cybersecurity architectures for industrial systems, where operational reality matters more than assumptions.

I work on the strategic and technical foundations of cybersecurity in safety-critical industrial environments.

My focus is on OT architectures that hold up when enterprise IT requirements collide with long system lifecycles, constrained connectivity, and operational priorities in industrial environments.

Rather than isolated controls, I focus on foundations: identity, trust, network boundaries, and risk management models that can be sustained by the line organization over time.

Areas of focus

  • Industrial identity and access foundations for OT system lifecycles
  • Risk management through assessments and targeted technical controls
  • Network boundaries and segmentation patterns in industrial environments
  • Translating regulatory intent (for example NIS2) into operational engineering
